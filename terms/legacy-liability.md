# Legacy Liability

> The structural condition of a business that has grown too dependent on human-centric coordination to rebuild itself without dismantling the organisation — the accumulated cost of every architectural decision made for human execution rather than agentic execution.

## Extended Definition

Legacy Liability is not a technology problem. It is an organisational one. A large incumbent did not arrive at its current state through poor decisions — it arrived through decades of rational ones, each optimised for the operating environment at the time. The middle-management layer was added because coordination required it. The approval chain was built because accountability required it. The reporting structure was designed because visibility required it. Each layer made sense when it was added. Collectively, they form a structure that cannot be made autonomous without first being dismantled — and most organisations will not authorise that dismantlement.

## Related Terms

- [Coordination Tax](https://arcoventure.studio/lexicon/coordination-tax) — Legacy Liability is the organisational accumulation of decades of Coordination Tax: the coordination infrastructure that was rationally built layer by layer cannot be removed without dismantling the organisation.
- [Operational Arbitrage](https://arcoventure.studio/lexicon/operational-arbitrage) — Legacy Liability is what prevents incumbents from capturing the Operational Arbitrage an autonomous competitor captures: the cost of rebuilding exceeds the cost of continuing to operate the legacy structure.
- [Automated Business](https://arcoventure.studio/lexicon/automated-business) — An automated business is a Legacy Liability business with technology overlaid: the human-centric coordination architecture remains intact beneath the efficiency tools applied on top.
- [Agentic Core](https://arcoventure.studio/lexicon/agentic-core) — The Agentic Core is the architectural alternative to Legacy Liability: a library of solved autonomous infrastructure problems that replaces the need to build from legacy foundations.
- [Infrastructure Drag](https://arcoventure.studio/lexicon/infrastructure-drag) — Infrastructure Drag and Legacy Liability describe the same structural cost from opposite directions: Legacy Liability is accumulated in incumbents, Infrastructure Drag is the upfront cost of avoiding it in a new build.
